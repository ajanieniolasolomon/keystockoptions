Genres of Trading Options Explained Take time to know about binary trade:

High/Low Call Put

This is probably the easiest and the simplest option for trading. The investor only needs to predict whether the price of the asset is going to rise or fall within a given time. The investor then selects Call if the prediction is a rise in price and Put if it is a fall.

One Touch

In this option, the investor predicts that the price of the asset will touch a specific value before the end of the given time. One Touch Option For example, the trading asset is EUR/USD valued at 1.3500 on Friday. A trading platform such as 7-traders.com can give the investors two options. The call option; meaning that the price of the asset will rise and reach 1.3800 at least once in the next week. The put option; meaning that the price of the asset will fall and reach 1.3200 at least once during the next week. In case you use a call option or a put option and the price touches the specified price then you win.

No Touch

It works the same way as the CALL/PUT option only that in this case, you select the price at which the asset must not reach before the selected period. Example: Google’s share price is $540 and the trading platform is on the No Touch price of $570 with percentage returns of 77 %. If the price does not reach 570 dollars after the given time, then you have a gain.

Option+

The option comprises prediction of a rise (Call) or a fall (Put) in the value of the asset in 30 seconds.
